📋 Citation Cheat Sheet

📘 Book
Format: Last, First. Title of Book. Edition (if applicable), Publisher, Year.
Example: Lucas, Stephen E. The Art of Public Speaking. 13th ed., McGraw-Hill, 2020.
🌐 Website
Format: Last, First. "Article Title." Website Name, Publisher, Date, URL.
Example: Smith, James. "History of Olympic Games." Olympic.org, IOC, 2023, olympic.org/history.
📰 Journal
Format: Last, First. "Article Title." Journal Name, Vol. #, Year, pp. #–#. DOI.
Example: Nolen-Hoeksema, Susan. "Eating Disorders Review." Annual Review of Clinical Psychology, Vol. 12, 2016, pp. 181–212.

🎤 Verbal Citation Formula

Always include all 3 elements out loud:
👤 Author's Name 📰 Title of Work 📅 Date Published
✅ "According to [Author] in [Title], published in [Year]..."
✅ "As [Author] reports in [Title] ([Year])..."
✅ "In [Year], [Author] wrote in [Title] that..."
❌ "Studies show..." (no author, no title, no date)
❌ "A researcher said..." (vague — costs you citation points!)
⭐ Remember for your outline: Label each piece of evidence with its type AND source. Example: (statistic, Lucas) — that's what your professor is looking for!
